Current regime for gold

BEARISH near-term

5 of 6 cyclical drivers are pushing gold down (hawkish Fed, rising real yields, surging dollar, fading safe-haven, cheaper oil). The lone structural support is central-bank demand — the floor. The whole regime turns when the Fed/rates leg turns; everything else follows that.

Side-channels: Iran de-escalation → oil −2% → safe-haven premium fades (adds to ④). Central-bank buying pushes the other way — the structural floor under ④. Note: oil is a counter-force to the dollar (cheaper oil is dollar-negative), but the Fed leg is overwhelming it.

How you'll tell it's flipping — the watchboard

DriverNowPressure on goldWhat flips it (trigger)Where to watch
The single best early tell: a bullish divergence — when the DXY keeps rising but gold stops falling. That means price-insensitive buyers (central banks) are absorbing the selling. Confirm it with real yields stalling or oil firming underneath. That divergence usually marks the bottom before the headlines turn.
