# The Russia–Ukraine War: An Objective, Sourced Briefing (as of June 2026)

> **Purpose & method.** This report was produced with a deep-research workflow: six parallel research streams (origins, military situation, diplomacy, humanitarian/economic impact, future of warfare, and media bias) each fanned out across web sources, prioritizing primary documents (treaty texts, UN/OHCHR/ICC/World Bank records, official statements) and reputable independent/OSINT outlets (ISW, Mediazona, BBC Russian, Bellingcat, RSF, CPJ). Every claim is tagged **VERIFIED** (corroborated across independent sources), **DISPUTED** (sources conflict or it is framing-dependent), or **ANALYTIC JUDGMENT/PROJECTION** (expert assessment, not settled fact). For contested points, the **Russian / Ukrainian / Western-neutral framings are presented side by side.**
>
> **How to read "objective fact" here.** On a contested war, very few headline numbers are clean facts. The most reliable category is institutional/primary records (treaty texts, ICC warrants, UN votes, OHCHR floors). The least reliable is belligerent casualty claims — both sides inflate enemy losses by roughly an order of magnitude, and both treat their own losses as state secrets. Where a number is a single-source estimate or a modeled figure, it is flagged.
>
> **One hard caveat on this run:** automated full-page fetching (`WebFetch`) was blocked (HTTP 403) for many primary outlets — ISW, Mediazona, Meduza, UN, Kremlin, Al Jazeera — so some exact digits rest on search-engine summaries of those primary sources rather than the opened page. The cited URLs are the primary sources and should be opened directly to confirm precise figures before any high-stakes use.

## Executive summary

- **Origins are genuinely contested in framing, but several "facts" are settled:** Russia annexed Crimea by force in 2014 (Putin later admitted deploying troops after first denying it), the UN General Assembly declared the annexation invalid, and the ICJ found "no evidence" for Russia's "genocide" justification for the 2022 invasion. Whether NATO enlargement *caused* the war is the central scholarly dispute — most mainstream analysts say no (it denies Ukrainian agency and Russia's stated goals exceeded any defensive rationale); a credible Western minority (Mearsheimer, Sachs) and the Russian state say yes.
- **The war is a grinding attritional stalemate.** Russia holds ~19–20% of Ukraine; the front (~1,000 km) is largely static. Russia took Pokrovsk and Huliaipole in early 2026 but its rate of advance collapsed — from ~9.8 km²/day (early 2025) to ~2.9 km²/day (early 2026) — and ISW recorded a Russian **net territorial loss** in spring 2026, the first since 2024.
- **Casualty figures are the least trustworthy data in the war.** Best independent estimates put Russian dead at roughly **332,000–500,000** (modeled) and Ukrainian dead at roughly **55,000 (official) to ~100,000–140,000 (independent)**. Both governments' claims about the *enemy* (Ukraine: ~1.37M Russian losses; Russia: ~1.5M Ukrainian) are inflated propaganda.
- **Diplomacy intensified sharply in 2025–26 but produced no settlement.** A Trump-brokered peace push (a 28-point plan cut to ~19 points), a European counterproposal, a "coalition of the willing," and repeated ceasefire attempts all ran aground on two irreducible obstacles: **territory** (Russia demands full Donbas; Ukraine refuses to cede held land) and **NATO/Western troops** (Russia rejects both). A US-set June 2026 deadline passed without a deal.
- **The future-of-warfare verdict:** drones (FPV, fiber-optic, naval), electronic warfare, and mass production now define the battlefield in an unstable action-reaction race no side durably dominates. **Who has the advantage is itself contested:** most Western think tanks say Russia is "losing strategically" (catastrophic losses for negligible gains, straining economy); credible analysts counter that Russia's mass and Ukraine's manpower/aid fragility favor Moscow over time. The honest answer is that the outcome hinges on **which structural constraint binds first** — Ukrainian manpower and Western aid, or Russian economic and equipment exhaustion — not on battlefield maneuver.
- **On media bias (your specific question):** Russian state media demonstrably contradict forensic ground truth (denying Bucha, MH17). Western/Ukrainian coverage is far closer to verifiable reality on atrocities and attribution, **but is itself documentably biased** on battlefield optimism (the over-hyped 2023 counteroffensive), uncritical acceptance of Ukrainian loss claims, and early hero-myths (the "Ghost of Kyiv," "Snake Island 13"). Ukraine's own press environment is genuinely constrained (the state "telethon," excluded opposition channels). **Net: no single outlet gives you unfiltered truth — triangulating OSINT (ISW, Bellingcat, Mediazona) against multiple national presses gets closest.**

## 1. Origins & Causes

### Settled facts
- **Ukraine surrendered the world's 3rd-largest nuclear arsenal** (~1,900 warheads) under the **1994 Budapest Memorandum**, in exchange for Russia/US/UK pledges to respect its sovereignty and borders and not use force against it. **[VERIFIED — primary treaty text]** ([UN treaty record](https://treaties.un.org/Pages/showDetails.aspx?objid=0800000280401fbb), [memo text](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/sites/default/files/documents/semon9-giki0/1994-12-05-Budapest-Memorandum.pdf))
- **2014: Euromaidan → Yanukovych's ouster → Crimea → Donbas.** Protests began Nov 2013 after Yanukovych suspended the EU agreement; deadly crackdowns followed; on 21–22 Feb 2014 he fled and parliament voted him out. Russia then seized Crimea with unmarked troops. **[VERIFIED]** ([Britannica](https://www.britannica.com/topic/Ukraine-crisis))
- **Putin first denied, then admitted, deploying Russian troops to Crimea** — explicitly in a 2015 documentary ("I gave orders... to deploy special forces of the GRU"). **[VERIFIED — documents a reversed denial]** ([RFE/RL](https://www.rferl.org/a/from-not-us-to-why-hide-it-how-russia-denied-its-crimea-invasion-then-admitted-it/29791806.html), [WaPo](https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/putin-changes-course-admits-russian-troops-were-in-crimea-before-vote/2014/04/17/b3300a54-c617-11e3-bf7a-be01a9b69cf1_story.html))
- **UNGA Resolution 68/262** declared the Crimea referendum/annexation invalid (100–11–58). **[VERIFIED]** ([UN](https://press.un.org/en/2014/ga11493.doc.htm))
- **The Donbas war (2014–2021) killed ~14,200–14,400**, including ≥3,404 civilians (incl. the 298 MH17 deaths); civilian deaths fell sharply after 2015. **[VERIFIED — OHCHR]** ([OHCHR report](https://www.ohchr.org/sites/default/files/2022-03/33rdReportUkraine-en.pdf))
- **July 2021: Putin's essay "On the Historical Unity of Russians and Ukrainians"** argued the two are "one people" and Ukrainian statehood is artificial — read by analysts as ideological groundwork for invasion. **[VERIFIED — primary text]** ([Harvard HURI](https://www.huri.harvard.edu/news/putin-historical-unity))
- **24 Feb 2022:** Putin announced a "special military operation" to "demilitarise and denazify Ukraine" and stop an alleged "genocide" in Donbas, also citing NATO expansion. **[VERIFIED — his stated words]** ([Kremlin transcript](http://en.kremlin.ru/events/president/news/67843), [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2022/2/24/putins-speech-declaring-war-on-ukraine-translated-excerpts))
- **The "denazification" and "genocide" justifications fail on the evidence.** Hundreds of Holocaust scholars called "denazification" "factually wrong, morally repugnant" (Ukraine's president is Jewish); the **ICJ (2 Feb 2024) found "no evidence" substantiating Russia's genocide allegations.** **[DISPUTED claim; independent-evidence consensus runs against Russia]** ([ICJ](https://www.icj-cij.org/node/203515), [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2022/03/01/1083677765/putin-denazify-ukraine-russia-history))

### The contested core — three framings

**Did NATO enlargement cause the war?** **[DISPUTED — the central scholarly dispute]**
- **Russian framing:** US-led enlargement, especially NATO's 2008 Bucharest pledge that Ukraine "will become" a member, crossed a red line and provoked a defensive response.
- **Ukrainian / mainstream-Western framing:** This erases Ukrainian agency; the cause is Russian imperial revanchism. Ukraine was nowhere near NATO membership in 2022, and Russia's *stated* aims (regime change, denial of Ukrainian nationhood) far exceed any defensive NATO rationale.
- **Western revisionist minority (Mearsheimer, Sachs):** A broader informal Western assurance was given; Russian resentment is genuine and was predictable.
- Sources: [LSE on the "betrayal myth"](https://blogs.lse.ac.uk/politicsandpolicy/exposing-the-myth-of-western-betrayal-of-russia/), [Mearsheimer](https://www.mearsheimer.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/06/Why-the-Ukraine-Crisis-Is.pdf), [Miller Center](https://millercenter.org/war-ukraine-fault-west), [NATO Bucharest text](https://www.nato.int/en/about-us/official-texts-and-resources/official-texts/2008/04/03/bucharest-summit-declaration)

**The "not one inch" pledge.** James Baker did tell Gorbachev in Feb 1990 NATO would move "not one inch eastward" — **[VERIFIED from declassified memcons]** — but its meaning is **DISPUTED**: historians (Sarotte) find no binding, written, general non-expansion agreement was ever concluded; the phrase referred to East German territory and was walked back. ([National Security Archive](https://nsarchive.gwu.edu/briefing-book/russia-programs/2017-12-12/nato-expansion-what-gorbachev-heard-western-leaders-early))

**Was Yanukovych's ouster a "coup"?** **[DISPUTED characterization]** Russia calls it an illegitimate Western-backed coup; Ukraine/West call it the "Revolution of Dignity," noting parliament — including many of his own party — voted him out.

**The Minsk agreements (2014/2015) and why they failed.** **[VERIFIED they existed and failed; sequencing DISPUTED]** Ukraine wanted ceasefire and border control first, then limited autonomy; Russia wanted Ukraine to grant Donbas broad constitutional autonomy first, while denying it was even a party to the conflict. The deliberate ambiguity made implementation impossible. ([ECFR post-mortem](https://ecfr.eu/article/ukraine-russia-and-the-minsk-agreements-a-post-mortem/))

## 2. Current Military Situation (mid-2026)

- **Russia occupies ~19–20% of Ukraine; the ~1,000–1,100 km front is largely static.** **[VERIFIED — broad consensus]** ([Russia Matters](https://www.russiamatters.org/news/russia-ukraine-war-report-card/russia-ukraine-war-report-card-may-20-2026))
- **Russia captured Pokrovsk and Myrnohrad (Donetsk) by late Jan 2026** but failed to exploit the breakthrough; **captured Huliaipole (Zaporizhzhia) Jan 2026.** **[VERIFIED — ISW/DeepState]** ([ISW](https://www.criticalthreats.org/analysis/russian-offensive-campaign-assessment-june-1-2026))

- **Ukraine liberated much of Kupyansk (Nov 2025)** and advanced in the Sumy/Borova directions; NATO's Rutte said Ukrainian defenses are stabilizing the front (21 May 2026). **[VERIFIED — ISW]** ([ISW](https://www.criticalthreats.org/analysis/russian-offensive-campaign-assessment-may-22-2026))

## 3. Diplomacy, Peace Efforts & Sanctions

- **Trump's 28-point plan (Nov 2025)**, drafted by envoy Witkoff with Kushner/Rubio input, was widely seen as favoring Russia: Ukraine cedes more Donbas, constitutionally renounces NATO, caps its military, de facto recognition of Russian-held territory, sanctions lifted, Russia returns to the G8. ([Axios](https://www.axios.com/2025/11/20/trump-ukraine-peace-plan-28-points-russia), [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2025/11/21/trumps-28-point-ukraine-plan-in-full-what-it-means-could-it-work))

- **France/Germany/UK issued a less pro-Russian counterproposal** (no extra territorial cession, higher military cap, no US control of frozen funds). ([Kyiv Independent](https://kyivindependent.com/trump-quite-pleased-with-ukraine-peace-talks-as-europe-reportedly-issues-counterproposal/))
- **A "coalition of the willing" (~35 countries, Paris, Jan 2026)** discussed a ~15,000–20,000-troop reassurance force with a US "backstop"; analysts question whether "Article 5-like" guarantees are credible without US combat forces. ([Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/1/6/ukraine-talks-in-paris-yield-significant-progress-on-security-pledges))

- **Territory:** Russia demands Ukraine fully withdraw from the Donbas it still holds and recognize Crimea; Ukraine refuses to cede held land ("we stand where we stand"; ceding would require a referendum "that would not happen").
- **NATO / Western troops:** Russia demands neutrality and no Western troops; Ukraine's NATO course is constitutionally enshrined.

## 4. Humanitarian & Economic Impact

### Human toll **[VERIFIED — primary institutions]**
- **OHCHR verified ~60,659 civilian casualties** (15,850 killed, 44,809 injured) since 24 Feb 2022 — and explicitly states the **true toll is "considerably higher"** (a floor, not a count). Civilian casualties rose ~21% in early 2026 vs 2025. ([OHCHR](https://ukraine.ohchr.org/en/reports))
- **~5.9 million Ukrainian refugees** globally and **~3.7 million internally displaced**; **10.8 million people need humanitarian aid in 2026.** ([UNHCR](https://data.unhcr.org/en/situations/ukraine), [IOM](https://crisisresponse.iom.int/response/ukraine-crisis-response-plan-2026))

### War-crimes allegations **[ICC warrants VERIFIED to exist; underlying guilt legally untested / Russia rejects ICC jurisdiction]**
- **ICC arrest warrants for Putin and Lvova-Belova (Mar 2023)** for unlawful deportation of Ukrainian children; further warrants for Shoigu, Gerasimov, and others (2024) for attacks on Ukraine's energy grid. ([ICC](https://www.icc-cpi.int/situations/ukraine))
  - *Western/ICC:* war crimes. *Ukrainian:* 19,553+ children "deported." *Russian:* "outrageous," a "humanitarian effort," rejects ICC jurisdiction.
- **Bucha (Mar–Apr 2022): VERIFIED as having occurred** with Russian forces responsible per UN/HRW/forensic and NYT satellite analysis (bodies present during Russian control); **Russia calls it "staged"** — a claim debunked by Bellingcat and the Centre for Information Resilience. ([OHCHR](https://www.ohchr.org/en/press-releases/2022/12/un-report-details-summary-executions-civilians-russian-troops-northern), [Bellingcat](https://www.bellingcat.com/news/2022/04/04/russias-bucha-facts-versus-the-evidence/))
- **Kakhovka dam (6 Jun 2023): RESPONSIBILITY DISPUTED** — the most genuinely unresolved attribution. Ukraine and the European Parliament call it a deliberate Russian act; some independent analysts attribute it to collapse from Russian negligence; engineering studies (Nature) found pre-collapse structural deformation; Russia denies. The dam was under Russian control, but **definitive forensic attribution remains unestablished.** ([Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s43247-024-01284-z))
- **Systematic strikes on Ukraine's energy grid (every winter 2022–2026): VERIFIED as occurring; legality DISPUTED.** UN/EU call them unlawful; Russia frames them as "retaliation."

### Economic impact **[VERIFIED]**
- **Reconstruction need: ~$588B over the next decade** (World Bank RDNA5, Feb 2026) — ~3× Ukraine's GDP; direct physical damage >$195B. ([World Bank](https://www.worldbank.org/en/news/press-release/2026/02/23/updated-ukraine-recovery-and-reconstruction-needs-assessment-released))

- **Energy shock:** Brent spiked to ~$139/bbl (Mar 2022); Russia cut ~80 bcm of pipeline gas to Europe (it had supplied ~44% of EU gas pre-war); the EU mobilized €650B+ and launched REPowerEU. ([ECB](https://www.ecb.europa.eu/press/economic-bulletin/focus/2022/html/ecb.ebbox202204_01~68ef3c3dc6.en.html))

## 5. The Future of Warfare — and Who Has the Advantage

### The drone-defined battlefield **[VERIFIED facts + ANALYTIC judgments]**
- **FPV drones now dominate the close fight**, creating a ~25 km "kill zone"; estimates attribute up to ~80% of frontline casualties to them (an estimate, not a measured figure). ([NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/03/17/nx-s1-5743446/russia-ukraine-war-nets-drones))
- **Fiber-optic drones (jam-immune, cable-guided, ~50 km)** are the response to electronic warfare — first Russian (2024), then Ukrainian. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fiber_optic_drone))
- **Ukraine inflicted a historically decisive asymmetric naval defeat** — ~30% of the Black Sea Fleet destroyed/damaged, the flagship *Moskva* sunk, the grain blockade broken — *despite having no conventional navy.* In April 2026 a naval drone even launched an interceptor that downed a Shahed (a first). ([Euromaidan](https://euromaidanpress.com/2025/12/24/ukraine-naval-drones-black-sea-fleet-group-13/), [Breaking Defense](https://breakingdefense.com/2026/04/in-a-first-ukraines-drone-force-launches-interceptor-drone-from-usv-to-destroy-shahed/))
- **Cheap interceptor drones now down Shaheds at scale** (>70% of Kyiv-area Shahed kills early 2026; ~$1,000–2,500 each vs millions per Patriot round). ([TWZ](https://www.twz.com/news-features/inside-ukraines-interceptor-drone-innovations-swatting-down-thousands-of-shahed-drones))
- **Electronic warfare made the spectrum a primary battlefield** (Russian Krasukha-4, Zhitel), driving the fiber-optic/autonomy arms race — an action-reaction loop with no stable equilibrium. ([CSIS](https://www.csis.org/analysis/russia-ukraine-drone-war-innovation-frontlines-and-beyond))
- **Both sides scaled production into the millions** (Ukraine claims ~8M FPV/year capacity for 2026; Russia targets ~7M+ and ~1,000 Shaheds/day). True coordinated AI swarms are still emerging — "first to field swarms gains major advantage." ([Just Security](https://www.justsecurity.org/138164/ukraine-drone-superpower/))
- **Ukraine's deep-strike campaign materially hit Russian oil refining in 2026** (≥21 strikes in April; crude processing reportedly at its lowest since 2009; strikes reaching >1,500 km). ([RFE/RL](https://www.rferl.org/a/ukraine-russia-drone-deep-strikes-oil/33751182.html))

### Attrition: the real contest
- **Manpower:** Ukraine faces an acute manning crisis — though analysts (Carnegie) argue it's a *systemic recruitment/retention* problem, not raw demographics. Russia relies on paid contract soldiers (recruitment fell ~20% in Q1 2026, requiring higher bonuses) plus ~12,000–15,000 North Koreans. ([Carnegie](https://carnegieendowment.org/research/2026/03/ukraine-military-russia-war-manpower-recruitment))
- **Industrial base:** Russia out-produces the West in artillery (~4:1 per NATO's Rutte; ~7M rounds in 2025), supplemented by ~12M North Korean shells (though 2025 NK volumes reportedly fell). The West targets ~3M 155mm for 2026. ([MWI](https://mwi.westpoint.edu/the-industrial-window-of-war-how-to-measure-russias-munitions-throughput-and-how-to-disrupt-it/))
- **Cost vs gain:** CSIS estimates ~420,000 Russian casualties in 2025 for ~0.8% more Ukrainian territory; advances of ~15–70 m/day (slower than the Somme). Russia's war economy is straining (2025 growth ~0.6%, fuel disruptions, rising personnel costs). ([CSIS](https://www.csis.org/analysis/russias-grinding-war-ukraine))

### Who has the advantage? — present both, declare neither **[ANALYTIC JUDGMENT / contested]**
- **"Russia is losing strategically" school (ISW, CSIS, RUSI, UK govt):** A declining power trading immense losses for negligible gains; strained economy and recruitment; Ukraine's drone/interceptor/deep-strike edge and Black Sea success show asymmetric leverage; Russia turns to hybrid escalation *because* it's failing conventionally.
- **"Time and mass favor Russia" school:** Russia keeps the initiative on priority axes, out-produces the West in shells and total drones, can absorb losses via contract pay + North Korean manpower, and is grinding through Ukraine's harder constraint — manpower. Attrition logic is "who can sustain losses longest," and Ukraine's mobilization crisis plus uncertain US aid are its key vulnerabilities.
- **Analytic consensus (mid-2026):** A war of attrition with **no imminent decisive breakthrough.** The trajectory looks increasingly unfavorable to Russia at the *strategic* level even as it grinds forward *tactically*. The honest bottom line: **the outcome hinges on which structural constraint binds first — Ukrainian manpower/Western aid or Russian economic/equipment exhaustion — not on battlefield maneuver.** This is a forecast, not a fact. ([Re:Russia](https://re-russia.net/en/analytics/0376/), [Cipher Brief](https://www.thecipherbrief.com/russias-war-ukraine))

## 6. Media Coverage & Bias — Is Reporting Close to Reality?

> Your specific question. The even-handed answer: **all sides distort, but not equally or in the same way.** Russian state media deny verified atrocities; Western/Ukrainian media are closer to ground truth on facts but biased toward optimism and Ukrainian sourcing; Ukraine's own press is genuinely constrained. **No single source gives unfiltered truth — triangulation is the method.**

### Western media — closer to truth on facts, but documentably biased
- **Coverage skews heavily toward the Ukrainian perspective**, with "war journalism" framing dominating (multiple academic content analyses). **[VERIFIED as a study finding]** ([CJR](https://www.cjr.org/the_media_today/media_bias_ukraine_war.php))
- **The 2023 counteroffensive was over-hyped** by Western media and officials, later acknowledged as a failure of expectation-management. **[VERIFIED]** ([WaPo](https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2023/05/06/ukraine-counteroffensive-expectations-hype-russia/))
- **Western media often accepted Ukrainian claims of Russian losses uncritically** — characterized by one academic as part of a Ukrainian morale-boosting effort. **[VERIFIED characterization; figures unverifiable]**
- **Pro-Ukraine myths Western media amplified, later debunked:** the **"Ghost of Kyiv"** (Ukraine's Air Force admitted it was fictional) and the **"Snake Island 13"** (reported killed, later found alive and captured). **[VERIFIED]** ([France24](https://www.france24.com/en/tv-shows/truth-or-fake/20220502-the-myth-behind-the-ghost-of-kyiv))

### Russian state media — demonstrably contradicts ground truth
- **Legally required to call it a "special military operation";** Roskomnadzor ordered deletion of "invasion"/"war." The March 2022 "fake news" law (up to 15 years) drove out independent media; TV Rain, Meduza, Novaya Gazeta, Echo of Moscow were blocked/labeled. **[VERIFIED]** ([CSIS](https://www.csis.org/analysis/russias-crackdown-independent-media-and-access-information-online), [HRW](https://www.hrw.org/news/2022/03/07/russia-criminalizes-independent-war-reporting-anti-war-protests))
- **Russia ranks 172nd in press freedom**, among the world's worst jailers of journalists. **[VERIFIED — CPJ/RSF]** ([CPJ](https://cpj.org/2026/02/russias-repression-record/))
- **State media denied Bucha and MH17** — both contradicted by satellite/forensic/OSINT evidence; the Dutch court convicted three men for MH17. **[VERIFIED]** ([Bellingcat](https://www.bellingcat.com/news/2020/03/10/a-post-mortem-of-russias-claim-that-crucial-mh17-video-evidence-was-falsified/))
- **EUvsDisinfo catalogs 15,000+ pro-Kremlin disinformation cases** (note: EUvsDisinfo is an EU body, not a neutral third party). ([EUvsDisinfo](https://euvsdisinfo.eu/))

### Ukrainian media — genuinely constrained
- **A single state-coordinated "United News" telethon (Marathon)** consolidated TV news in 2022; RSF and Ukrainian monitors criticize it as overly pro-government (a survey found 62% of journalists consider it a form of censorship); three opposition-linked channels were excluded. **[VERIFIED]** ([RSF](https://rsf.org/en/ukraine-rsf-calls-government-put-end-telemarathon))
- A 2024 EU enlargement report urged restored media pluralism. Still, Ukraine *rose* to 61st in RSF's index, and most press deaths there are attributed to Russian fire. **[VERIFIED]**

### Global South / non-aligned
- **Chinese state media** avoid "invasion," echo "special military operation," and cast the US/NATO as instigator domestically. **[VERIFIED]** ([Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2022/4/6/china-media-coverage-ukraine-war))
- **Al Jazeera and much Global South coverage** adopt a more "non-aligned" frame foregrounding diplomacy and double-standards critiques. **[DISPUTED/interpretive characterization]**

### The honest bottom line on bias
**[Component facts VERIFIED; the comparison is analytical synthesis]** The documented *scale, state-coordination, and institutional fabrication* of disinformation are substantially greater on the Russian side (denying verified atrocities). Western/Ukrainian distortions tend toward *exaggeration and over-optimism* (inflated enemy losses, hero-myths, battlefield hype) rather than atrocity denial. **To get closest to ground truth, weight independent OSINT (ISW, Bellingcat, Mediazona, OHCHR) above any national press, and read at least two adversarial national presses against each other.**
