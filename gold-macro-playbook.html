How to position right now

Regime: BEARISH near-term, structurally bullish

Hawkish Fed + surging dollar are pressuring gold now; central-bank demand is the long-term floor. You're over-concentrated (~50%+ metals) and underwater. The plan separates the de-risk-now moves from the wait-for-the-flip moves.

1

Trim 25–35% of GLD + SLV & harvest the losses do now

Highest-conviction move — it's risk management + tax alpha, not a market call. Offsets your ~$100k realized gain. Swap to IAU/SIVR to stay exposed; respect the 61-day wash-sale window (no buying GLD/SLV shares or calls, no selling puts — including the long leg of any spread).

2

Redeploy proceeds into de-correlated assets do now

Broad equities, international, quality/dividend, TIPS, cash/T-bills. Avoid more metals, miners (GDX), and crypto — same factor.

3

On the retained metals core: measured income or protection while bearish

If you expect grind-sideways-to-down: covered calls / a small bear-call-spread for income + partial hedge (disciplined strikes, don't over-cap your recovery). If you fear a real leg down: a collar protects far more than premium. Mind the wash-sale interaction with step 1.

4

Flip to offense when the Fed-pivot triggers fire the signal

When 2–3 of these hit: Dec-hike odds fall <61% · 2-yr yield rolls over · DXY closes <100 · bullish divergence (DXY up, gold refuses to fall) — unwind the bearish overlays and rebuild the metals core. Your $450 breakeven sits below every bank's end-2026 target, so the long-term thesis stays intact.

One-line version: de-risk & harvest now (it's not a market timing call), collect income/protect the core while the regime is bearish, and lean back into gold when the Fed-pivot dials turn. Educational, not advice.
