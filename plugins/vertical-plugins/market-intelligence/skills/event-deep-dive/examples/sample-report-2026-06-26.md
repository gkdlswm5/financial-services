## The one-paragraph read

The tape is being driven almost entirely by a **macro regime shift, not by business deterioration**: a new Fed chair (Warsh) turned hawkish on June 17 — the dot plot flipped from a cut to signaling a *hike* (median end-2026 dot 3.4%→3.8%), the 2-year yield had its biggest Fed-day jump since 2008, and an energy-driven inflation spike (May CPI +4.2%) priced cuts out. Simultaneously the **Iran war round-tripped to peace** (Islamabad Memorandum, June 17; Hormuz reopened), collapsing oil ~40% and crushing gold/silver. Through a Buffett lens almost all of this is **NOISE** — it moved *quotes and discount rates*, not *owner-earnings*. The signal sits underneath: **banks cleared the stress test and are raising capital returns, energy supermajors got cheaper on a sanctions headline while their dividends/buybacks compound, and Broadcom lost $285B on a tone while its AI revenue grew 143%.** That gap — crowd selling on macro mood vs. businesses unchanged — is the whole opportunity set.

## 5. Data-quality / honesty flags

The adversarial pass **refuted 3 claims and flagged 4 as uncertain** — surfaced rather than buried:

- ❌ **Silver "fifth straight deficit year"** → it's the **sixth** (Silver Institute World Silver Survey 2026, ~46.3 Moz). Bank gold targets ($6,000–6,300) held up.
- ❌ **Nvidia FY26 net income "$120.07B / +64.8%"** → actual ~**$117.0B / +58%**. (Revenue $215.9B and 75% Q4 GM were correct.)
- ❌ **Nvidia "$58.32B GAAP net income" in Q1 figure** → GAAP diluted EPS was $2.39, not $1.87 (the $1.87 is the *non-GAAP* EPS, which was correct).
- ⚠️ **Strait-of-Hormuz closure date** ("March 27 IRGC closure") — sources put effective closure from ~Feb 28 / confirmed early March.
- ⚠️ **JPM/SOX dates** — TSM's +6.86% pop was June 19 (Fri), not June 21 (Sun); SOX "−10% then rebound" chronology is loose.
- ⚠️ **Univest GLD/SLV −1.8%/−4.3%** figures are India-listed GoldBees/SilverBees, though magnitudes match US GLD/SLV.
- ⚠️ Single-source items (J.P. Morgan ~755t CB-gold forecast; some sector-split details) are plausible but uncorroborated.

## Bottom line (the Buffett takeaway)

1. **Don't trade the macro.** Rates, oil, gold/silver, FX — all NOISE on the owner-earnings test; un-forecastable, outside the circle. The heat strip's value is knowing *which way the crowd is leaning so you can fade it*, not predict it.
2. **The signal is capital returns.** Banks (post-stress-test buybacks/dividends) and the energy majors (39-yr Chevron streak, Exxon's $20B buyback at depressed prices) are *quietly* compounding per-share value while the crowd looks elsewhere.
3. **The opportunities are in the OWN IT corner** — businesses sold on macro mood, not deterioration: the Broadcom overreaction, the Exxon/Chevron oil-headline drop, the rate-driven index dip. Buy unchanged businesses when the quote falls.
4. **Respect the FALLING KNIFE.** Oil and silver are hated *and* deteriorating — wait for the cycle, don't anchor on the wartime highs.
5. **Nvidia is the honest dilemma:** real, improving owner-earnings — but *dearer*, with a moat hard to underwrite at the multiple. Great business ≠ great price.
