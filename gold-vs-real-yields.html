How to read it & why it matters

How to read it

  • Lines together = inverse confirmed. Through 2024–25, real yields fell and gold soared — the textbook relationship.
  • The right edge is the story. In 2026 real yields spiked (hawkish Warsh Fed) and gold rolled over — that's today's headwind, drawn in red.
  • Watch for the lines to re-converge. When real yields stop rising and turn down, gold's tailwind returns.

Why this is THE relationship

  • Gold pays no interest. Its only "cost" is the real (after-inflation) return you give up by not holding bonds.
  • So the real yield is gold's opportunity cost — the truest single driver, cleaner than the dollar or nominal rates.
  • If you watch one number for gold's direction, watch the 10-yr real yield (FRED: DFII10).
The tell that matters for you: right now real yields are climbing and gold is falling — fully consistent, no surprise. Your flip signal is when this chart's lines start re-converging (real yields stall/turn down). That typically leads the bullish turn — and lines up with the Fed-pivot triggers (Dec hike odds <61%, DXY <100). Educational, not advice.
