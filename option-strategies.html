The 3 strategies — and which view each fits

StrategyStructureEffectBest when you…
① Stock repair (1×2 ratio)Buy 1 ATM call, sell 2 OTM calls (~$0 cost)Lowers breakeven; recovers loss in a "sweet spot"; caps upsideexpect a moderate grind back up
② Covered callSell 1 call above your basisCollects premium (income), lowers breakeven a bit; caps upside at strikeare patient & want income while waiting
③ Collar (protective)Buy a put, sell a call to fund it (~$0)Floors downside, caps upside — insurance, not recoveryfear the hawkish-Fed downside

GLD — basis $450 · 1,100 sh (11 contracts)

Hold ① Repair ($390 / 2×$430) ② Covered call ($480) ③ Collar ($370p / $460c)

SLV — basis $75 · "large position"

Hold ① Repair ($60 / 2×$70) ② Covered call ($80) ③ Collar ($54p / $78c)

note Silver is higher-beta: strategy payoffs are wider and IV is richer, so the repair/covered-call premiums work harder — but a sharp silver drop hurts more. Forecasts are also more dispersed (HSBC ~$57 bear vs Commerzbank $90 bull).
