Why gold is moving — drivers (editorial · updated Jun 22, 2026)

🦅
Hawkish Fed surprise under Warsh now dominant Warsh's first FOMC stripped forward guidance and he declined to submit a dot (first in 14 yrs); 2026 dots now imply ~+25bps with 9 of 18 officials seeing a hike. A stronger dollar followed — this reversed the peace-deal rally and sank gold below $4,200.
🕊️
US–Iran interim deal faded Turned out to be an MOU — a 60-day negotiating window that sets up (not yet completes) the Hormuz reopening. The initial ~3% oil-driven rally above $4,345 has unwound as the conditional nature sank in.
🏦
Central-bank demand — record survey structural floor WGC 2026 Reserves Survey: a record 45% of central banks plan to add gold over the next 12 months. PBoC buying 8t in April. This is the anchor holding the structural floor as rates pressure mounts.
📈
Sticky inflation backdrop bullish May CPI 4.2% / PPI +6.5% YoY still elevated; gold remains the finite-supply inflation hedge even as the energy spike unwinds.
⚖️
Tug-of-war: hawkish Fed vs. sovereign bid watch Near-term direction hinges on whether higher real-rate pressure (bearish) or price-insensitive central-bank buying (bullish) wins. Analysts (Heraeus) flag medium-term pressure from the hawkish Fed + Iran de-escalation.
